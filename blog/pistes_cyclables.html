Cette piste est un en danger: Bike lanes and the 2025 mayoral election

2025.11.10

Last week, Projet Montréal lost control of the city council for the first time since 2017 after 8 years of attempts at reducing car traffic, improving pedestrian and cyclist infrastructure, and reversing urban sprawl.

Mayor-elect Soraya Martínez-Ferrada's Ensemble Montréal won on a platform focused on improving vehicle commuting and lane expansion in the city, including an oft-repeated audit of all the city's bike lanes her the first 100 days of office, citing concerns about efficiency and safety. Ensemble Montreal is committed to repurposing bike lane funding, reducing pedestrianized streets, and ensuring Montreal's car culture thrives. Hooray.

Ensemble Montreal has since walked back their promise of a bike lane audit, but the writing is on the wall: public sentiment towards bike lanes is more negative than ever.

My instinct is to advocate for pistes cyclables dans toutes les rues de Montréal, but let's look at the data. Are Montrealers justified in electing a council party who aims to reduce cycling infrastructure?

The short answer is: Kind of.
